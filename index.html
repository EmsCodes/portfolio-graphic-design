Semester-project

Rebranding of a local jazzfestival, "Sortland jazzfestival". (images and text for the assignment was borrowed from them)

The project assignment was to choose a graphic design project for any client (real or fictional), and create at least four printed design products, and a brand style guide. The designs had to be print ready. I chose to rebrand Sortland Jazzfestival. And made a new logo, poster, flyer and brochure.

I based my design on Sortland as the "blue city", and the original "jazz era", the 1920-30s, and the art deco style. I wwanted it to be a bit fun, luxurious and exciting at the same time. I created illustrations of three well known buildings in Sortland to create even more "ownership" to the locals, since they present the majority of the audience.

Course assignment - Book cover

The assignment was to choose from 4 books, and design a cover using one or more gestalt principles.

I chose "Frankenstein", by Mary Shelley. I chose a dark color and design, to match the story and feeling. Created a tree with the shape of frankensteins face in it, and added a church than can bee seen because of the moon, using the figure ground principle.

Course assignment - Logo

We had to choose between a few companies and create a logo, and a brand styleguide. We also had to create a gif. I chose to create a logo for a japanese textile/fabric shop, located in Netherlands. I tried to combine the traditional japanese with the modern Dutch, to bring the two contries "toghether".

Course assignment - Recipe booklet

We had to create a recipe booklet for children. I tried to create an exciting and fun booklet, that could be used by children and their parents.

Photo - Course Assignment

The assignment was to take 16 photos, using only the settings on the camere to adjust/create the photos we wanted. We also had to create them based on different criteria, like black on black, contrast, movement, new perspetive, low light, negative space.

I learned a lot about composition and how to take good photos tweaking the adjustments available on the camera.
